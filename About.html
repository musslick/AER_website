Autonomous EmpiricalResearch.ai

Autonomous Empirical Research is an open source AI-based system for automating each aspect empirical research in the behavioral sciences, from the construction of a scientific hypothesis to conducting novel experiments.

Introduction

Accelerating Empirical Science with Machine Learning

The integration of behavioral phenomena into computational models of human cogniton is a fundamental staple of empirical research in neuroscience and psychology. Yet, researchers are beginning to accumulate increasing amounts of data without having the temporal, computational or monetary resources to integrate these data into scientific theories, and/or to test resulting theories in follow-up experiments. Autonomous Empirical Research  seeks to revolutionize the empirical research process in cognitive neuroscience and psychology, by incorporating existing machine learning techniques into a closed-loop system for the generation, estimation and experimental validation of scientific models.

Applications

Key beneficiaries include empirical scientists who seek to advance their research at a faster pace without sacrificing reproducibility and transparency. A behavioral research study with five successive experiments and 500 participants would take a human researcher about 20 weeks to complete using traditional methods. Autonomous empirical research could assist a human researcher to accomplish the same study in less than a week while ensuring quantitative rigor, reproducibility and transparency. In addition, autonomous empirical research can be applied in domains of industry where companies seek to capitalize on internal data, e.g. in user experience research.


Approach

The core system consists of two competing artificial agents: (a) an autonomous theorist that constructs computational models, linking experiment conditions to dependent measures; and (b) an autonomous experimentalist that derives predictions from these models, and designs novel experiments to test them. The competitive interaction between the autonomous theorist whose objective is to build models based on available data, and the autonomous experimentalist whose objective is to invalidate these models by collecting new data, implements a form of adversarial curiosity (Schmidhuber, 2020).

Methods

Autonomous Empirical Research leverages techniques that emerged from the past years of research in machine learning and computer science, such as

Neural Architecture Search & Symbolic Regression

for automating the discovery of interpetable model architectures.

Automatic differentiation

for automating the fitting of model parameters to experimental data.

Artificial Curiosity & 
Active Learning

for identifying novel experiment conditions that yield scientific merit. 

SweetPea

for automating experimental design.

Dallinger

for automating the collection of behavioral data on Amazon's Mechanical Turk.
